Day 7: Persevering Through Opposition

← Previous Day Day 7 ← Series Home

Scripture Reading

Behold, I am sending you out as sheep in the midst of wolves, so be wise as serpents and innocent as doves. Beware of men, for they will deliver you over to courts and flog you in their synagogues, and you will be dragged before governors and kings for my sake, to bear witness before them and the Gentiles. — Matthew 10:16-18 (ESV)

Devotional

As Jesus concludes His instructions to the disciples, He gives them a sobering reality check: "I am sending you out as sheep in the midst of wolves." He doesn't sugarcoat the challenges they will face. He doesn't promise that everyone will welcome their message or appreciate their ministry. Instead, He prepares them for opposition and persecution. This honest assessment is actually an expression of Jesus's love for His disciples. He doesn't want them to be surprised or disillusioned when they face resistance. He wants them to be prepared, to understand that opposition is not a sign of failure but an expected part of faithful witness. The teaching points out that Christians throughout history and around the world today face various forms of persecution for their faith. In some places, this includes imprisonment, torture, or even death. In other contexts, the opposition might take the form of ridicule, exclusion, or discrimination. Jesus wants His followers to expect opposition so they can persevere through it. But Jesus doesn't just warn the disciples about opposition; He also gives them guidance for navigating it: "Be wise as serpents and innocent as doves." This memorable phrase calls for a balance of strategic wisdom and moral integrity. The disciples are to be shrewd in understanding the world around them, anticipating challenges, and finding effective ways to share the gospel. At the same time, they are to maintain their integrity, not compromising their witness through deception, manipulation, or retaliation. The message emphasizes that opposition often creates unique opportunities for witness. Jesus tells the disciples they will be "dragged before governors and kings for my sake, to bear witness before them and the Gentiles." What appears to be a setback from a human perspective can be a divine setup for the gospel to reach people who might not otherwise hear it. Throughout the book of Acts and church history, we see how persecution often served to spread the gospel further and wider than before. This perspective transforms how we view opposition. Rather than seeing it as something to avoid at all costs or as evidence that we're doing something wrong, we can see it as an expected part of faithful witness and even as an opportunity for the gospel to advance in new ways. Today, consider how you respond to opposition or resistance when you share your faith. Do you retreat in fear, or do you persevere with wisdom and integrity? Do you see opposition as an obstacle to be avoided or as an opportunity for witness? Ask God to give you the courage and perspective you need to faithfully represent Christ, even when it's difficult or costly.

Reflection Questions

  1. What forms of opposition or resistance have you experienced when sharing your faith? How did you respond?
  2. What does it look like in your context to be "wise as serpents and innocent as doves"?
  3. How might viewing opposition as an opportunity rather than just an obstacle change your approach to sharing Christ with others?
  4. How has God helped you persevere through difficult situations?
  5. What encouragement can you take from Jesus' promise that the Holy Spirit will give us the words to speak?

Prayer

Lord Jesus, thank You for Your promise to be with us even in the midst of opposition. Help me to persevere in sharing Your love, no matter what challenges I face. Give me wisdom and discernment in how to respond to those who reject Your message. Help me to remember that You are always with me and will give me the words to speak when I need them. In Your name, Amen.

Today's Application

Write down three specific ways you can prepare yourself to persevere in sharing your faith, even when it's difficult. Consider practical steps like memorizing key Bible verses, building a support network, or developing a regular prayer habit for strength and encouragement.
